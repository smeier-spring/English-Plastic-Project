Make the Choice to Stop Buying Single Use Plastics

Our consumption of single use plastics is a serious problem. According to the University of Colorado, 150 million tons of single use plastic are produced every year, which is around the same weight as 411 Empire State Buildings, 14,851 Eiffel Towers, or 1,724 Taj Mahals. Because single use plastic is designed to be single use and cannot be recycled, this 150 million tons or 1724 Taj Mahals worth of plastic, cannot be recycled must be thrown away. Single use plastic ususally takes 200 to 500 years to break down, and even then, it does not decompose. Instead, the tiny bits of microplastics wind their way into the ocean, the soil, animals, and even our digestive systems.

The clock is ticking...

The amount of pollution we are generating is piling up rapidly and it will not be long before the monster of excess waste that we have created rushes up to swallow us all.

Your Actions Matter

Our politicians and corporations are not doing anything to act despite being in positions of power and influence to do so. Therefore, change must begin with you and your choices, or otherwise it will not begin at all.
Make the choice to stop buying and consuming single use plastics.

"If we want to move towards a low-polluting, sustainable society, we need to get consumers to think about their purchases." -David Suzuki, environmental activist, former professor at the University of British Columbia

You might be thinking to yourself that one person stopping their single use plastic consumption does not do anything, because there are still companies producing single use plastics and millions of people out there buying and throwing away that single use plastic every single day. And to an extent, you are correct. When you choose to stop consuming single use plastics, our wasteful society will still go on. What you may have forgotten, however, is the principle of the compound effect, or the idea that small changes consistently over time lead to unexpectedly dramatic results.
When you stop consuming plastic, the anti-plastic movement spreads.
Action by action, example by example, person to person, it spreads.
And so long as the movement is spreading, as more and more people stop buying and consuming single use plastics, society will be forced to adapt to be less wasteful.

While larger scale environmental issues like biodiversity loss and carbon emissions are very important, it is much harder for the average person to take direct action on it as opposed to reducing their plastic consumption, which anyone can do if they have determination and empathy for the earth.

This is Peanut.

As a baby, a red eared slider turtle accidentally trapped herself in a plastic ring that was littered into her natural environment. Because she could not get out of the plasticas when she grew to adulthood, her shell became warped into a figure eight shape, hence why she was named "Peanut" when people found her.

All it took was the decision of one person to buy and throw away a single use plastic item to disfigure this turtle for life.
